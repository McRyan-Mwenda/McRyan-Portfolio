My journey

I used to want to become a professional rugby player. But soon after completing my highschool I realized I had a passion for coding after being introduced to a C++ tutorial by a friend.

Given my new found passion, the next logical step for me was to join university and pursue a course which would allow me to learn more about coding and creating software. I joined the Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology; however I soon felt dissatisfied with my learning experience in university. I therefore dropped out and decided to become my own teacher.

Quitting university obviously came with a lot of backlash from friends and family and as a result I developed a deep depression and self-doubt that crippled me for several years. I lost all confidence in myself and my capabilities. This was a hard time for me. I felt lost and devoid of purpose.

In the recent past however, I have been fortunate enough to get help and mentorship from a very good counsellor. With her assistance I rediscovered my passion for software development and I was able to develop clarity of where I was at the moment and where I wanted to be in future.

Soon after I began my self-education and self-development journey. In the past year, I have learned so much – about myself, coding and software development, the world, writing, speaking, and so much more.

One of the things that has become apparent to me throughout this journey, is that if you have a dream and make achieving your dream your top priority, nothing will stand in your way.

Today, I am a confident fullstack web developer, an entrepreneur and a pretty good writer – however my journey of self-development and self-improvement doesn't stop there, it is a journey I shall continue embarking on in all dimensions of my professional, personal, and inter-personal life.
